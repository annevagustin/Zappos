How to Care for a Llama

  1. Give your llama a good shelter. It should be at least three sided with a high roof for ventilation.
  2. Feed your llama. Read more here.
  3. Like a horse, give your llama ground mineral salt (llama's can not lick like a cow or horse so a block won't work) in his/her stable which s/he can access whenever needed.
  4. Have a suitable fence for your llama. The best kind of fencing to use is corral fencing, do not use cheap picket fencing, rotted fencing, or barbed wire fencing.
  5. Make sure that there is nothing dangerous in the shelter and pasture area, such as broken glass, or poisonous plants.
  6. Get your llama sheared at least once a year as to keep them cool and their wool clean.
  7. Llama feet are very sensitive, and have nails which need to be clipped. If you do not know how to clip your llamas feet, hire a professional as trying on your own with no experience can hurt your llama.
Source: WikiHow.com
